What is Dolomite? What is Dolomite Powder?

What is dolomite? Dolomite is a mineral and rock composed of calcium magnesium carbonate. The dolomite formula (dolomite chemical formula) is CaMg(CO₃)₂. Dolomite powder meaning (dolomite meaning in industry): the fine white powder made by grinding dolomite rock, used as a source of magnesium and calcium and as a functional filler. As a dolomite mineral, it has a rhombohedral crystal structure and often forms when magnesium replaces calcium in limestone. Dolomite powder is produced by crushing and grinding dolomite rock to a fine white powder. It is widely recognized for its strength-enhancing properties, cost-effectiveness, and chemical stability. Industries worldwide use dolomite powder as a functional filler in paints, plastics, construction, glass, and rubber.

Dolomite is a mineral and rock made of calcium magnesium carbonate. The dolomite formula (dolomite chemical formula) is CaMg(CO₃)₂. Dolomite mineral forms in sedimentary rocks and is a key source of magnesium. Dolomite powder is ground dolomite used as a filler in paints, plastics, construction, glass, and rubber. Dolomite vs limestone: limestone is mainly CaCO₃; dolomite contains both calcium and magnesium carbonate.

The main uses of dolomite (and dolomite powder) are: construction (cement, concrete, building materials), paints and coatings (extender pigment), plastics (filler for stiffness and cost reduction), glass and ceramics (flux, magnesium source), rubber (tires, industrial rubber), and agriculture (soil conditioner, magnesium supplement). Dolomite uses in agriculture include correcting acidic soil and supplying magnesium to crops.

Dolomite vs limestone: Limestone is primarily calcium carbonate (CaCO₃). Dolomite is calcium magnesium carbonate (CaMg(CO₃)₂). Dolomite contains magnesium and is harder and often more resistant to weathering. Limestone is more common and used for cement, lime, and aggregates; dolomite is preferred where magnesium content or higher strength is needed—e.g. construction, glass, and some industrial fillers.
