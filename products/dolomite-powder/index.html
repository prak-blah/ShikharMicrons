Dolomite Powder

Dolomite Powder is a double carbonate of calcium and magnesium with a unique crystalline structure. It is used as a filler in paints, plastics, rubber, and ceramics, and is valued for its high purity, whiteness, and ability to improve product strength and durability. We supply consistent particle size distributions across 100–1000 mesh for reliable processing and end-product performance.

Applications

  • Paints and coatings (dolomite powder for paint)
  • Plastic and polymer industry
  • Rubber industry
  • Ceramics and glass
  • Construction materials

Industry Solutions

  • Paints & Coatings: 300–600 mesh for balanced opacity, flow and scrub resistance. See: Dolomite Powder for Paint.
  • Plastics: 400–600 mesh improves stiffness and dimensional stability while optimizing cost.
  • Rubber: 200–400 mesh enhances tensile strength and abrasion resistance.
  • Ceramics & Glass: Provides magnesium and calcium source for improved firing and durability.

Properties

  • High purity and whiteness
  • Good dispersibility
  • Improves mechanical properties
  • Low moisture content
  • Stable chemical composition

Technical Specifications

CaCO3 + MgCO3 Content ≥ 97.5%
Whiteness ≥ 92%
Mesh Sizes Available 100, 200, 300, 400, 500, 600, 800, 1000
Particle Size Range 7-8 microns (100 mesh) to 15 microns (1000 mesh)
Moisture Content ≤ 0.3%
Oil Absorption ≤ 28 g/100g
pH Value 8.5 - 9.5

Frequently Asked Questions

Dolomite offers better mechanical strength, improved fire resistance, and enhanced durability in polymer applications. It's also more cost-effective for applications where maximum whiteness isn't critical.

Construction materials, ceramics, glass manufacturing, and rubber industries benefit most from dolomite powder due to its strength-enhancing properties and cost-effectiveness.

Dolomite powder improves tensile strength, impact resistance, and dimensional stability in polymers. It also reduces costs while maintaining product quality.

For paint applications, 400-600 mesh dolomite powder (15-25 microns) provides optimal coverage and finish quality. We offer sizes from 100 mesh (7-8 microns) to 1000 mesh (15 microns) to meet various application requirements.
