What is Calcite Powder?

Calcite powder is a natural form of calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) derived from the crushing and processing of high-grade calcite rocks. It is widely recognized for its exceptional brightness, smooth texture, and superior chemical properties. Due to its unique characteristics, industries worldwide use this fine white powder as a filler and coating agent in their manufacturing operations.

Uses of Calcite Powder in Various Industries

Our calcite powder serves as a versatile filler and functional additive across multiple industries:

Paint and Coating Industry

In paints and coatings, calcite powder acts as an extender pigment that improves opacity, reduces costs, and enhances durability. It provides excellent coverage, weather resistance, and helps control viscosity for better application properties. Learn more: Calcite Powder for Paint.

Plastic and Polymer Industry

As a functional filler in plastics, calcite powder improves mechanical properties, reduces material costs, and enhances dimensional stability. It's widely used in PVC pipes, profiles, films, and injection-molded products. Learn more: Calcite Powder in Plastic Industries.

Paper Manufacturing

In paper production, calcite powder serves as a filler and coating pigment, improving brightness, opacity, and printability while reducing production costs and environmental impact. Learn more: Calcite Powder in Paper Industries.

Rubber Industry

Rubber manufacturers use calcite powder to improve processing characteristics, enhance mechanical properties, and reduce costs in tire manufacturing, footwear, and industrial rubber products. Learn more: Calcite Powder in Rubber Industries.

Ceramics and Glass

In ceramics and glass manufacturing, calcite powder acts as a fluxing agent, improving melting characteristics and final product quality while maintaining cost efficiency.
