Calcite vs Dolomite Powder Comparison

Understanding the key differences between calcite powder and dolomite powder for optimal application selection.

Calcite Powder
  • • Pure CaCO₃ (Calcium Carbonate)
  • • Higher brightness (95%+)
  • • Lower oil absorption (25-28 g/100g)
  • • Better for paints & coatings
  • • Superior whiteness & opacity
  • • Excellent for paper industry
  • • Higher cost, premium quality
Dolomite Powder
  • • CaMg(CO₃)₂ (Calcium Magnesium Carbonate)
  • • Enhanced strength properties
  • • Cost-effective solution
  • • Better for construction materials
  • • Superior durability & hardness
  • • Excellent for glass industry
  • • Good for bulk applications

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about calcite powder, dolomite powder, and particle sizing

Formula: Microns = 25,400 ÷ Mesh Size
Example: 600 mesh = 25,400 ÷ 600 = 42.3 microns (theoretical)
Practical: 600 mesh = 25 microns (actual particle size)
Note: Practical values are more accurate for real-world applications of our calcite powder and dolomite powder.

Calcite Powder: Pure calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) with higher brightness (95%+) and lower oil absorption, ideal for paints and high-end applications.

Dolomite Powder: Calcium magnesium carbonate (CaMg(CO₃)₂) with enhanced strength properties and cost-effectiveness, perfect for construction and bulk applications.

200-300 mesh (25-50 microns): Construction materials, coarse fillers
400-600 mesh (15-25 microns): Paints, plastics, standard industrial use
800-1500 mesh (2-15 microns): Fine applications, high-end coatings, smooth finishes

Coated powders are surface-treated with stearic acid or other agents to enhance polymer compatibility, improve dispersion, and provide superior mechanical properties. Our coated calcite powder and coated dolomite powder offer better processing characteristics and reduced moisture absorption.

Smaller particles (higher mesh): Smoother finish, better opacity, higher cost
Larger particles (lower mesh): Better bulk properties, cost-effective, rougher finish
Consider: Application requirements, processing equipment, cost constraints, and final product specifications.
